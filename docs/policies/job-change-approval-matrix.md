# Job Change Approval Matrix

## When this policy applies

This policy governs every change to an active employment relationship that alters role, level, compensation, location, employment type, or reporting line. It does not apply to changes that are purely administrative (legal-name updates, address changes, emergency-contact updates) or that flow automatically from a previously approved decision (a vesting cliff, a scheduled merit increase under a Board-approved compensation cycle).

The policy exists to enforce two things. First, that the right approvers see the right changes before they become commitments to the employee — verbal promises made by managers without authority are the most common source of compensation disputes and retention drama. Second, that every change is documented in a form that survives manager turnover. If the manager who promised the promotion leaves before the paperwork is processed, the promotion still happens; if it was never in the system, it didn't happen.

Change requests are initiated by the employee's direct manager through Workday. The HR Business Partner is automatically routed onto every request and may add or escalate approvers based on the matrix below.

## Change types covered

**Promotion.** Any increase in level (L1 → L2, L4 → L5, and so on). Promotions require both a level change in the HRIS and, in nearly all cases, a compensation adjustment to bring the employee within the new level's band. Promotions outside the published annual promotion cycle are permitted but require Director sign-off in addition to the standard chain.

**Lateral move.** A change in role or team at the same level. Includes function changes (engineering to product, sales to customer success), team changes within the same function, and reporting-line changes that do not alter level. Laterals between functions also trigger a 90-day check-in with the receiving manager to confirm fit.

**Compensation adjustment.** Any change to base salary, target bonus, or commission plan that is not part of the annual cycle. Includes market adjustments, retention awards, and corrections to off-band placements identified through pay-equity review.

**Location change.** A move between work locations, including cross-country relocations, intra-country state or province changes that affect tax registration, and shifts between in-office, hybrid, and fully remote status where remote work materially changes the employment tax footprint. Cross-country moves always involve Legal and the Global Mobility partner regardless of level.

**Employment-type conversion.** Conversion between contractor and full-time employee, between full-time and part-time, between fixed-term and indefinite contract, or between employment in one legal entity and another within the corporate group. These are the highest-risk change category and are governed jointly by this policy and the *Contractor to Full-Time Employee Conversion* policy.

## Approval authority by level

The approval chain scales with the level of the employee whose role is changing, not the level of the manager initiating the request. Each approver in the chain must approve in sequence; an approval cannot be skipped, though a single approver may delegate within their reporting line with written notice to HR.

| Employee level | Promotion / lateral | Compensation adjustment (within band) | Compensation adjustment (above band) | Location / employment-type change |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| L1 (Associate) | Manager + HRBP | Manager + HRBP | Director + VP + CHRO | Director + HRBP |
| L2 (Mid) | Manager + HRBP | Manager + HRBP | Director + VP + CHRO | Director + HRBP |
| L3 (Senior IC) | Manager + Skip-level + HRBP | Manager + HRBP | Director + VP + CHRO | Director + VP + HRBP |
| L4 (Staff / Manager) | Skip-level + Director + HRBP | Manager + Director + HRBP | VP + CHRO | VP + HRBP + Legal (cross-country) |
| L5 (Senior Staff / Senior Manager) | Director + VP + HRBP | Director + HRBP | VP + CHRO | VP + CHRO + Legal |
| L6 (Principal / Director) | VP + CHRO | VP + HRBP | CHRO + CEO | CHRO + Legal |
| L7 (Senior Principal / VP) | CEO + CHRO | CEO + CHRO | CEO + CHRO + Board Comp Committee | CEO + CHRO + Legal |

"HRBP" means the named HR Business Partner for the receiving organization. "Skip-level" means the manager of the employee's direct manager. "Within band" means the new compensation falls within the published band for the employee's level after geo-adjustment; see *Compensation Bands*.

## Escalation thresholds

The matrix above is the default. The following conditions add approvers regardless of level:

- **Compensation more than 10% above the band midpoint for the level** — requires CHRO sign-off, even for changes that would otherwise be Manager + HRBP.
- **Compensation more than 25% above the band ceiling** — requires CEO sign-off in addition to CHRO.
- **Cross-country moves at any level** — Legal and Global Mobility are required co-approvers. Tax, immigration, and entity-presence considerations frequently block or reshape the move; HR cannot complete one of these without their sign-off.
- **Equity refresh outside the annual cycle** — requires VP + CHRO at all levels. Refreshes inside the annual cycle follow the Board-approved equity grant guidelines and do not require per-employee approval beyond the manager's recommendation.
- **Any change affecting an employee on a Performance Improvement Plan** — requires CHRO sign-off. Promotions and out-of-cycle compensation increases are not permitted during an active PIP except in narrow correction-of-error cases approved by Legal.
- **Any change affecting an employee under jurisdictional stability protection** — pregnancy leave, parental leave, Brazil CIPA / union mandate, Germany severe-disability status — requires Legal review before being routed for approval.

## Documentation required

Every change request must include the following in the Workday ticket before it can be routed: the business justification (one paragraph minimum, explaining the why, not just the what); the new role description for promotions and laterals; the proposed effective date; the new compensation breakdown (base, variable, equity), benchmarked against the published band; the impact on team headcount budget; and, for cross-country moves, the tax-residency analysis from Global Mobility.

The HRBP is responsible for confirming completeness before the request is routed to the first business approver. Incomplete requests are returned to the manager rather than partially approved.

## Timelines and SLAs

Standard turnaround targets, measured in business days from full ticket submission:

- **Within-band compensation, lateral, promotion (L1–L3):** approval within 5 business days, effective on the next pay-period boundary.
- **Above-band compensation, promotion (L4–L5):** approval within 10 business days, effective on the next pay-period boundary or as specified.
- **L6 / L7 changes:** approval within 15 business days; effective dates frequently align with quarterly cycles for reporting reasons.
- **Cross-country relocation:** 30 to 60 business days end-to-end, driven by visa and entity-setup timelines, not HR processing.
- **Employment-type conversion:** 10 to 20 business days, with country-specific timelines in the conversion policy.

The manager communicates the change to the employee only after the full approval chain has signed off. Verbal commitments to employees in advance of approval are the single most common source of escalations to the CHRO and are not consistent with this policy.
